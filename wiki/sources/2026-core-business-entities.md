# Core Business Entity Catalog

**Source:** raw/assets/core_business_entity.docx
**Ingested:** 2026-04-22
**Type:** note (entity catalog — intended design for build phase)

## Summary

This document catalogs every core data entity in the Multi-Tenant Adaptive Lead Intelligence Engine, organized in three groups. For each entity it states: what it is, and why it is needed. The document is the authoritative list of what must exist in the data model to support the full system — lead lifecycle, governance, and operational observability.

**Group A — Core Lead Lifecycle (15 entities):** tenant, business_profile, business_profile_source, ideal_customer_profile, lead, contact_identity, lead_source, event, lead_enrichment, enrichment_source, signal, signal_evaluation, lead_score, score_model_version, consent_preference.

**Group B — Governance & Quality (6 entities):** audit_log, tenant_policy, quality_rule, quality_check_result, feedback_record, lineage_record.

**Group C — Operational Observability & Delivery (11 entities):** business_profile_version, ideal_customer_profile_version, pipeline_run, task_execution, telemetry_event, alert_incident, slo_measurement, dashboard_definition, notification_delivery, delivery_endpoint, report_definition.

The entity catalog connects directly to the existing wiki documentation: `signal` and `signal_evaluation` are the data model backing the fill-in-the-blanks prompt mechanism; `lineage_record` is the entity behind [[concepts/lineage-log]]; `pipeline_run` and `task_execution` are the data backing the governance monitoring domain.

## Key Claims

- **`signal` is a formal entity:** the evaluation questions generated by the Signal Agent from the ICP, organized by dimension (fit, intent, context, behaviour, engagement). Not just a prompt concept — a stored, queryable record.
- **`signal_evaluation` is a formal entity:** the per-lead answer or result for each signal question after checking the lead's enriched data. This is the data that fills in the blanks in the scoring prompt.
- **`score_model_version` enables reproducibility and rollback:** tracks which version of scoring logic (thresholds, model, schema) produced a given score. Maps to `schema_version` + `prompt_version` fields in the Intelligence Layer's ScoringOutput.
- **`contact_identity` is the deduplication anchor:** normalized phone, email, and social identifiers for a lead, ensuring the same person across multiple channels is treated as one identity.
- **`consent_preference` is non-negotiable for delivery:** even a high-quality HOT lead should only be contacted through allowed and preferred channels. Consent must be checked before any outreach action.
- **`pipeline_run` gives workflow-level visibility:** one record per complete workflow execution (ingestion, enrichment, scoring, report generation). Tells you whether the full business process succeeded for a tenant, not just whether individual steps fired.
- **`task_execution` gives step-level visibility:** one record per stage inside a pipeline run. Most failures happen at the step level, not the whole pipeline level — this entity identifies exactly where.
- **`business_profile_version` and `ideal_customer_profile_version` enable historical explainability:** which business understanding and ICP definition were active when a signal set and score were produced. Enables Pipeline 2 re-run impact analysis.
- **`slo_measurement` closes the observability loop at the reliability level:** tracks whether service-level targets (scoring latency, processing completion, report delivery time) are being met — not just whether errors occurred.
- **`lineage_record` is the data model for full provenance:** links source objects/fields to target objects/fields through enrichment, scoring, aggregation, and reporting. Answers "where did this output come from and how was it produced."
- **`feedback_record` makes feedback a first-class system input:** feedback on scores and recommendations is stored as structured data, not loose comments, so the platform can improve scoring quality over time.

## Questions Raised

- What is the exact schema for `signal` — does it include the dimension type (fit/intent/etc.) and the detection rule format? Detection rule format is a hard blocker for orchestration (see [[analyses/orchestration-layer-dependencies]]).
- Does `quality_rule` store the rule definition as SQL, as a threshold + field reference, or as free text? This determines whether quality checks are automated or manual.
- How does `report_definition` relate to `dashboard_definition` — are reports a subset of dashboard output, or separate delivery artifacts?
- What drives `alert_incident` creation — is it the governance layer monitoring job, or does `task_execution` failure trigger it directly?
